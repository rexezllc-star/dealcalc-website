How to Estimate Rehab Costs for a House

Use repair categories, cost per square foot, contractor bids, contingency, and DealCalc's rehab calculator to build a more reliable property rehab estimate.

What this guide helps you do

This guide explains the investment logic behind the related DealCalc tools. Use it to understand the formula, the assumptions, and the diligence questions before making a decision.

Key concepts

  • Start with conservative assumptions.
  • Separate value, income, expenses, financing, repairs, risk, and exit strategy.
  • Verify every input with comps, documentation, inspection, or professional review.
  • Use calculators for screening, not as a substitute for diligence.

Example workflow

Review the deal packet, extract the core numbers, run the relevant calculator, compare the output to your investment threshold, then identify the next diligence items that could change the decision.

Professional rehab cost workflow

Professional investors usually do not rely on one number. They start with a fast rehab cost estimator, then refine the budget with room-by-room repair categories, contractor pricing, and contingency planning. The goal is not to guess perfectly on day one. The goal is to avoid overpaying before inspection, title review, and contractor walkthroughs confirm the assumptions.

Common rehab cost categories

  • Roof, gutters, siding, exterior paint, and landscaping.
  • Kitchen cabinets, counters, appliances, flooring, and lighting.
  • Bathroom tile, vanity, plumbing fixtures, tub, shower, and ventilation.
  • HVAC, electrical, plumbing, windows, doors, flooring, and interior paint.
  • Permits, debris removal, contractor markup, holding costs, and contingency.

How much contingency should you add?

For light cosmetic work, many investors start with a 10% contingency. For moderate rehab, 15% is often more realistic. For heavy rehab, older houses, mechanical uncertainty, or projects requiring permits, 20% or more may be appropriate. A rehab calculator is helpful because you can test each scenario before making an offer.

FAQ

What is a rehab cost estimator?

A rehab cost estimator helps investors approximate repair costs before making an offer. It should be supported by comps, inspection notes, contractor bids, and a contingency reserve.

How do investors estimate rehab costs?

Investors usually combine cost-per-square-foot assumptions, room-by-room budgets, contractor bids, and line-item repair scopes.

What is the fastest way to estimate repairs?

The fastest method is cost per square foot, but the most reliable method is a detailed repair category budget validated by contractors.

Should I use the 70% Rule with rehab estimates?

Yes. Rehab estimates are a key input in the 70% Rule and MAO calculations because repairs directly reduce the maximum price an investor can pay.
