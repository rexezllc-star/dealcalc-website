How to Estimate Rehab Costs on a House Flip

Rehab estimates can make or break a flip. A deal that looks profitable on paper can fail quickly when repair costs are underestimated.

Start with categories, not guesses

Break repairs into exterior, interior, mechanical systems, and project costs. This prevents common omissions like permits, dumpsters, contractor markup, and contingency reserves.

Use cost per square foot as a reasonableness check

After building a category estimate, divide total rehab cost by square footage. A light cosmetic project may be very different from a full renovation, so compare the cost per square foot against condition level.

Always include contingency

A contingency reserve protects the deal from issues discovered after inspection, demolition, or contractor walkthroughs. If the property condition is uncertain, raise the contingency.

Common rehab estimate mistakes

  • Ignoring mechanical systems like HVAC, plumbing, and electrical.
  • Forgetting permits, dumpsters, and cleanup.
  • Using national averages without local contractor input.
  • Leaving out holding costs and buyer closing costs.
  • Failing to adjust MAO after repair risk increases.
