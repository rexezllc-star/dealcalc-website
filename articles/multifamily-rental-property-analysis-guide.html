How to Analyze a Duplex, Triplex, or Fourplex Investment Property

Small multifamily deals require better analysis than a single rent field. Unit count, vacancy, expense ratios, and financing all shape the deal.

Begin with gross scheduled income

Gross scheduled income is the rent you would collect if every unit were occupied and paying market rent, plus any other monthly income such as storage, laundry, or parking.

Adjust for vacancy

Effective gross income subtracts vacancy and credit loss from total income potential. Even fully occupied properties should be modeled with a vacancy reserve.

Calculate NOI

NOI equals effective income minus operating expenses before debt service. It is the foundation for cap rate and DSCR.

Review DSCR

DSCR compares NOI to annual debt service. A higher DSCR means the property income has more cushion to support financing.

Check cash-on-cash return

Cash-on-cash return compares annual cash flow to the total cash invested, including down payment, closing costs, and initial repairs.
